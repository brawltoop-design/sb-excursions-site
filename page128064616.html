What to expect on this Ubud and Kintamani day

Following pickup around 8 AM, the day moves through Bali's craft villages, sacred temples, mountain viewpoints, and the most iconic corners of Ubud. It is designed to feel easy and complete, with lunch, tickets, transport, and the main cultural stops already built into one professional route.

Highlights of this Bali experience

This is the kind of full-day itinerary travelers book when they want Bali's cultural mood, a real volcano view, and the best-known Ubud stops in one smooth day.

Mount Batur lunch viewpoint

Enjoy an Indonesian buffet lunch in Kintamani with panoramic views over Mount Batur and Lake Batur, one of the signature landscapes of central Bali.

Temple and heritage route

See atmospheric sacred sites including Batuan, Sebatu, and Gunung Kawi for a day that feels cultural as well as scenic.

Tegalalang and coffee tasting

Balance the temple route with famous rice terrace scenery, spice gardens, and a stop for Balinese Luwak coffee and ginger tea.

Ubud icons in one finish

Round out the day with Monkey Forest, Ubud Market, and Ubud Royal Palace, giving the experience a stylish and complete Ubud ending.

Sample itinerary

Stop order can shift with traffic, your hotel location, and weather, but this is the route style the source tour is built around.

Hotel pickup and artisan villages

Start with pickup from your accommodation and head toward Batubulan, Celuk, and Mas to see Batik weaving, silversmith work, and wood carving traditions.

Central Bali temples

Continue through Batuan and on to Sebatu and Gunung Kawi, where the route leans into sacred architecture, symbolism, and the calm rhythm of central Bali.

Kintamani volcano lunch stop

Pause in Kintamani for lunch with wide views over Mount Batur and its lake, adding a real volcano moment to the middle of the day.

Tegalalang, coffee, and Ubud

Finish with Tegalalang rice terraces, a coffee plantation visit, Monkey Forest, Ubud Market, and Ubud Royal Palace before the return drive.

FAQ

These are the most useful questions travelers ask before booking an all-day Ubud and Kintamani route.

What is included in this tour?

The usual package includes entrance tickets and fees, bottled water, buffet lunch, a driver-guide, hotel pickup and drop-off, transportation by air-conditioned minivan, and all taxes and handling charges.

What time is pickup?

Pickup normally starts around 8:00 AM. Exact timing depends on where you stay, so it is best to send your hotel or villa details when you request availability.

How long does the tour take?

This is a full-day Bali experience of around 10 hours, depending on your hotel area, traffic, and how much time you want to spend at each stop.

Is this tour good for couples, families or friends?

Yes. It is especially strong for couples, families, and friends who want one balanced day with cultural stops, scenic viewpoints, lunch, and easy logistics.

What should I bring?

Bring sunscreen, a hat, comfortable shoes or strapped sandals, a camera, and extra cash for drinks, tips, or souvenirs you may want during the day.

What happens if the weather is bad?

The day can still be enjoyable in mixed weather, but volcano views and stop order may be adjusted. If your plans change, the source operator states that cancellations made at least 3 days before the tour qualify for a full refund.
